Centerstage

The objective of the game was to score pixels on a backboard while also having robots on a strict height limit. Your team could score more points with making mosaics. Another way to score was with a "drone" which was a paper airplan that needed to be lauched a certain distance to get 30 more points

Check out our Centerstage robot!

Robot Parts

Intake

Our Intake was an active rake mechanism this year. Since the pixels were an odd shape we needed to devise a plan to pick them effectivly. This lead us to trying to rack them into a funnel that puts it on to our Tray CAD part.

Scoring

For scoring on the backboard we decided to first centeralize the pixels onto a certain area and then let them fall one by one. We also used 2 lift kits to lift the tray CAD part to the board.

CAD

This year's robot had multiple CAD parts which were the spool, tray, and side panels for our intake mech. The spool mostly tried to lift the robot as that was another way of gaining points. The tray was a part used to put the parts into a centeralized area to move to the board. Lastly the side pannels was another custom part used to hold the intake as close to the ground as possible.

Auto

This year we worked very hard towards having a successful autonomous program. Unfortunately, our efforts were stopped short, though we accomplished many things that will be useful to us in later seasons. We worked towards finishing a Kalman Filter that allowed us to take a sample of data, usually an image, and filter it to remove noise and get more precise data. We also created an autonomous program that allowed us to move anywhere on the field and implemented a coordinate system that allowed the robot to know where it was on the field at a given moment with relatively good precision.

TeleOP

This year we had very efficient TeleOP code that allowed us to have a quick cycle time for scoring. One obstacle we faced was defining limits for the motors so that our lift kits would not break. We also decided to automate certain processes in the TeleOP period using autonomous code. To make it easier for the drivers, we cubed the controller's sticks' input values to allow for more precise adjustments given smaller values.
